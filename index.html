LIFE BELOW WATER

SDG 14


Overview




"Protecting Our Oceans for Future Generations"

The world's oceans, seas, and marine resources are facing unprecedented threats, including pollution, overfishing, and climate change. The 14th Sustainable Development Goal, "Life Below Water", was established to address these challenges and ensure the long-term health and productivity of the ocean. This goal recognizes the critical importance of the ocean to human well-being, food security, and economic growth, as well as its role in regulating the climate and providing half of the oxygen we breathe.

With the ocean covering over 70% of the Earth's surface and supporting an estimated 200,000 identified species, the goal of "Life Below Water" is to conserve and sustainably use marine resources, reduce marine pollution, and protect marine ecosystems. By achieving this goal, we can help maintain the delicate balance of the ocean's ecosystem, support the livelihoods of millions of people who depend on the ocean, and ensure a healthy and productive ocean for future generations.

SDG 14 aims to conserve and use the oceans, seas, and marine resources in a sustainable manner. It prioritizes the prevention and reduction of marine pollution of all kinds, as well as to minimize and address the effects of ocean acidification and the regulation of fisheries.

Overall it aims to conserve and sustain resources within our waters by addressing marine pollution, the protection and restoration of our marine ecosystems, promotion of sustainable fishing, and the conservation of coastal and marine areas. The goal also emphasizes the combat of ocean acidification, support for small-scale fisheries, and implementation of international laws in order to protect marine biodiversity. SDG 14 aims to preserve the long-term health and productivity of our oceans for our future generations.

Issues and Solutions

Overfishing

Overfishing in the Philippines has been a problem due to the fact that over 75% of the nation’s fishing ground has been depleted. This threatens the nation’s food source and the livelihoods of many within the Philippines.

Solution:

Regulation and limitation of fishing within Philippine waters to allow fishes to breed and multiply. A movement called Fish Forever also aims to help fishermen manage and regulate fishing within Philippine waters.
